1. Sypnosis

Groupware@LES has collected data from accelerometers on belt, forearm, arm, and dumbull on 6 participants to predict how well they perform barbell lifts in 5 different ways. In our analysis, we built a prediction model, using Machine Learning techniques, to predict the â€œclasseâ€ variable in the dataset. In fact, the â€œclasseâ€" variable has values â€œAâ€, â€œBâ€, â€œCâ€, â€œDâ€, and â€œEâ€, with classe A (exactly according to the specification to) to classe E (throwing the hips to the front). From our exploratory analysis, cross validation technique, and resulting accuracy rates, we concluded that a Random Forest model will best predict â€œclasseâ€. We obtained an accuracy rate of 99.03%, while comparing our prediction to the testing set.

From calculation correlation coefficients, we observed that â€œroll-beltâ€ is highly correlated to â€œyaw-beltâ€: 0.8152297. In order to reduce variance and by observing which of these two variables is most fit for our model, we decided to keep roll_belt only.

Exploratory Analysis - Part 1

The â€œgyros-â€ variables measure the orientation for 3-axial raw signals in the X, Y and Z directions. After careful look at plots of these variables, we decided to remove them from our dataset besides â€œgyros-belt-y/zâ€ and â€œgyros/arm/yâ€. In fact, from observing exploratory plots, we did not see them fit for our model. However,we observed a nice linear relationship between â€œgyros-belt-y/zâ€ and â€œgyros-arm-yâ€ histogram showed a homogenic relationship with â€œclasseâ€.

For the â€œmagnet-â€ variables, after looking at histograms and correlations, we removed magnet-belt-x, magnet-arm-y, and magnet-dumbbell-x/y. In fact, we didnâ€™t observe any major trend with the â€œclasseâ€ variable.

We also removed variables with large number of NAs or zeros, time variables, non-total accel_ values, and column with certain indexes (X, _window).
